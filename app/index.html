Product philosophy

Useful software does not need to feel heavy.

Many everyday tasks benefit more from a focused utility than from a large platform. The goal is to build tools that are easy to understand, easy to return to, and confident about what they are designed to do.

Make the core job obvious

A product should communicate its purpose quickly and avoid making users search through unnecessary layers.

Keep the interface calm

Visual hierarchy, spacing, and motion should support the task instead of competing for attention.

Improve through real use

Products become better when friction is identified in real workflows and refined with deliberate changes.
