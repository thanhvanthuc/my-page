What this center is for

A simple privacy directory for independent software.

This page is not intended to replace each product's detailed privacy policy. Instead, it gives users one place to discover the relevant policy and understand the general structure behind how privacy information is presented across the site.

01

Product-specific information

Different extensions use different browser capabilities, so privacy information is separated by product and written around the actual features in use.

02

Readable explanations

Policies aim to explain practical behavior in clear language, including what data is processed temporarily, what may be stored locally, and what is not collected.

03

Direct access

Users can open the privacy page for a specific product directly from this directory without searching through unrelated legal or technical information.

Privacy approach

Transparency should stay close to the product.

For focused browser extensions, privacy information is most useful when it clearly describes what the extension actually does. The goal of these policy pages is to connect privacy statements directly to product behavior instead of relying on vague, generic language.

Explain data handling in context

When a product processes information to provide a feature, the related policy should describe why that processing happens and whether the information remains local.

Separate local storage from data collection

Saving settings or interface preferences on a device is different from transmitting user information to a remote service, and policies should make that distinction clear.

Keep product policies maintainable

As products evolve, dedicated policy pages can be updated independently so users can review information that remains relevant to the version of the extension they are using.

Provide a clear contact path

Users should have a direct way to ask questions about privacy, permissions, or how a specific product behaves.

How this privacy center works

One directory, separate policies, clearer context.

Each extension or digital product can have its own privacy page. This structure keeps the general privacy center easy to browse while allowing the detailed policy pages to explain technical behavior with the level of detail appropriate for each product.

Separate policies by product

Each extension or application can have its own privacy page with information tailored to its actual functionality, permissions, and storage behavior.

Clear data-use explanations

Policies explain what information is processed, stored locally, transmitted, or not collected at all, with language that stays connected to the product's real behavior.

Easy future expansion

As new extensions are released, their privacy policies can be added to this directory without making existing product policies harder to find.

Direct support contact

Questions about privacy or product behavior can be sent directly to the developer through the contact information provided across the site.

Privacy questions

Need clarification about a product policy?

For questions about how a specific extension handles information, permissions, local storage, or other privacy-related behavior, contact the developer directly and mention the product name in your message.
